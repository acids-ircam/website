9:T5be,Our hardware projects represent our commitment to bringing AI out of the computer and into the physical world of music-making. We believe that the tactile experience of interacting with physical controls is an essential part of musical expression, and our hardware designs aim to combine this physicality with the sonic possibilities of neural audio models.

FlowSynth addresses a common challenge in music production: recreating a specific sound heard in a recording using available synthesizers. By leveraging normalizing flows, a type of generative model, FlowSynth learns the relationship between synthesizer parameters and the resulting audio. When presented with a target sound, it can suggest parameter settings that produce similar sonic results.

Our hardware controllers for neural audio models provide musicians with physical interfaces for interacting with complex AI systems. These controllers translate familiar musical gestures—turning knobs, moving faders, playing notes—into parameters that control neural networks, creating an intuitive bridge between traditional musicianship and cutting-edge AI.

These projects demonstrate our approach to technology development: we start with the needs and practices of artists, then design technologies that enhance their creative process rather than disrupting it. By bringing AI into familiar musical contexts, we make these advanced technologies accessible to a wider range of musicians and sound
